# Michigan Real Estate Market Research

## Executive Summary

Michigan's real estate market offers a compelling balance of affordability and cash flow potential for investors with $100K capital. The state's median home price ($269,667) sits significantly below the national median ($357,000), creating accessible entry points for rental property investment.

---

## 2024-2025 Market Trends

### Key Metrics

| Indicator | Value | Trend |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Median Home Price | $269,667 | ↑ 4.7% YoY |
| Average Home Price | $245,716 | ↑ 4.3% YoY |
| Market Condition | Balanced | Stabilizing post-pandemic |
| Inventory | Tight | Seller advantage in key areas |

### Demographic Drivers

- **Population Growth**: Modest but positive, supported by international migration
- **Migration Patterns**: Detroit and Lansing attracting buyers from coastal markets seeking affordability
- **Buyer Demographics**: Millennials and Gen Z entering market in affordable mid-sized cities

---

## Target Cities Analysis

### 1. Detroit

**Why Detroit?**
- Most affordable major city in Michigan
- 2.0% projected price increase (2025)
- Economic revival with downtown diversification
- Highest cash flow potential

**Market Stats:**
- Cap Rates: 4.9% - 5.9% (multifamily)
- Rental Growth: 3.4% annually (2nd highest among top 50 US markets)
- Occupancy: Rising to 93.8% by end of 2025
- Rent Growth Forecast: 4.0% by Q3 2025

**Investment Considerations:**
- ✅ Very low entry prices
- ✅ Strong rental demand
- ✅ Potential for appreciation
- ⚠️ Neighborhood variance (research block by block)
- ⚠️ Higher property management needs

**Best Cash Flow Areas:**
- South Rockwood
- Lincoln Park
- Shelbyville (6.33% average cash-on-cash return)

---

### 2. Grand Rapids

**Why Grand Rapids?**
- 2.8% projected price increase (2025)
- Strong job market and population growth
- More stable than Detroit
- High tenant demand

**Market Stats:**
- Steady appreciation trajectory
- Lower vacancy rates
- Diverse economy (healthcare, manufacturing, tech)

**Investment Considerations:**
- ✅ More stable tenant base
- ✅ Better appreciation potential
- ✅ Growing economy
- ⚠️ Higher entry prices than Detroit
- ⚠️ More competitive market

---

### 3. Lansing

**Why Lansing?**
- State capital = stable government employment
- 60% appreciation over past 5 years
- One of Michigan's best residential investment markets
- Affordable with growth potential

**Investment Considerations:**
- ✅ Government-backed employment stability
- ✅ Strong recent appreciation
- ✅ Michigan State University nearby
- ⚠️ Smaller market, fewer properties

---

### 4. Ann Arbor

**Why Ann Arbor?**
- 1.2% projected price increase (2025)
- University of Michigan = constant rental demand
- High-income tenant base
- Resilient market

**Investment Considerations:**
- ✅ Consistent rental demand from students/professors
- ✅ High-income demographics
- ✅ Low vacancy rates
- ⚠️ Higher property prices
- ⚠️ Seasonal turnover (academic calendar)

## Financial Analysis

### Cap Rates by Property Class (Detroit Metro)

| Property Class | Cap Rate Range |
|----------------|----------------|
| Class A | 4.74% - 5.17% |
| Class B | 4.92% - 5.28% |
| Class C | 5.38% - 5.90% |

### Cash Flow Formula

```
Monthly Cash Flow = Rent - (Mortgage + Taxes + Insurance + Maintenance + Property Management)
```

### Typical Returns

- **Stable Rentals**: 6-10% annual returns
- **Cash-on-Cash Return**: Varies by financing (often need 35-40% down for sub-4% CoC)
- **Best Cash Flow Markets**: 6.33% average (South Rockwood, Lincoln Park, Shelbyville)

---

## Risks & Considerations

### Market Risks
- Rising insurance and maintenance costs compressing NOI
- Mortgage rate fluctuations affecting affordability
- Regional economic dependence on automotive industry

### Seasonal Factors
- Harsh winters impact construction timelines
- Higher maintenance costs (snow removal, heating)
- Slower tenant turnover in winter months

### Mitigation Strategies
- Budget 10-15% for maintenance reserves
- Focus on properties with recent mechanical updates
- Build relationships with reliable contractors
- Consider properties with separate utility metering
