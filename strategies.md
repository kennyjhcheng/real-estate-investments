# Investment Strategies

With **$100,000 in capital**, we have several strategic options for Michigan real estate investment. This document outlines potential approaches, their pros/cons, and recommendations based on our team's strengths.

## Strategy 1: Single-Family Rental (SFR) - Focus Approach

### Concept
Purchase 1-2 single-family homes in cash or with minimal financing, focusing on cash-flowing neighborhoods in Detroit suburbs.

### Capital Allocation
- **Property 1**: $60,000 - $80,000 (cash purchase + light rehab)
- **Reserve Fund**: $15,000 - $20,000
- **Operating Capital**: $5,000 - $10,000

### Target Profile
- 3 bed / 1-2 bath homes
- Working-class neighborhoods with stable employment
- Properties needing cosmetic updates only
- Target rent: $1,200 - $1,500/month

### Pros
- ✅ Simple to manage
- ✅ Strong tenant demand for SFRs
- ✅ Easier exit (sell to homeowner or investor)
- ✅ Lower tenant turnover

### Cons
- ⚠️ All eggs in 1-2 baskets
- ⚠️ Vacancy = 100% income loss
- ⚠️ Limited scale with $100K

### Best For
First investment to learn the market before scaling.

## Strategy 2: House Hacking (BRRRR Method)

### Concept
Purchase a multifamily property (duplex/triplex), live in one unit, rent others. Refinance to pull capital out and repeat.

### Capital Allocation
- **Down Payment (3.5-5% FHA)**: $10,000 - $15,000
- **Rehab Budget**: $20,000 - $30,000
- **Reserves**: $10,000
- **Remaining for Deal 2**: $45,000 - $60,000

### Target Profile
- Duplex or triplex in Detroit or Grand Rapids
- Value-add opportunity (cosmetic rehab)
- After-repair value increase of 20-30%

### The BRRRR Steps
1. **Buy**: Purchase with FHA loan (owner-occupied)
2. **Rehab**: Renovate using contractor connections
3. **Rent**: Lease all units at market rate
4. **Refinance**: Pull equity out via cash-out refi
5. **Repeat**: Use proceeds for next deal

### Pros
- ✅ Low down payment (FHA)
- ✅ Live rent-free or cash-flow while living there
- ✅ Scale faster with leverage
- ✅ Build portfolio with limited capital

### Cons
- ⚠️ Requires owner occupancy (1 year minimum)
- ⚠️ More complex financing
- ⚠️ Multifamily management complexity

### Best For
One partner willing to relocate to Michigan temporarily.

## Strategy 3: The "Detroit Cash Flow" Portfolio

### Concept
Purchase 2-3 lower-priced cash-flowing properties in Detroit's emerging neighborhoods.

### Capital Allocation
- **Property 1**: $35,000 purchase + $10,000 rehab = $45,000
- **Property 2**: $30,000 purchase + $10,000 rehab = $40,000
- **Reserves**: $15,000

### Target Profile
- Sub-$50K purchase prices
- Section 8 or working-class rentals
- Heavy value-add (contractor advantage)
- Target rent: $800 - $1,000/month per property

### Cash Flow Projection (Per Property)
| Item | Monthly |
|------|---------|
| Rent | $900 |
| Property Management (10%) | -$90 |
| Insurance | -$50 |
| Taxes | -$80 |
| Maintenance Reserve | -$90 |
| **Net Cash Flow** | **$590** |

### Pros
- ✅ Immediate cash flow
- ✅ Diversification across multiple properties
- ✅ Utilizes contractor advantage for value-add
- ✅ No mortgage = lower risk

### Cons
- ⚠️ Higher management intensity
- ⚠️ Lower appreciation potential
- ⚠️ Tenant quality variance

### Best For
Generating immediate passive income with less appreciation focus.

## Strategy 4: Partnership Syndication

### Concept
Partner with local Michigan investors on larger deals, contributing capital as limited partner.

### Capital Allocation
- **LP Investment**: $100,000
- **Target Deal**: 10-20 unit multifamily

### Structure Options

#### Option A: Silent Partner
- Contribute capital only
- Receive preferred return (8-12%) + equity split
- No active management

#### Option B: Active Partner
- Contribute capital + manage rehabs (contractor advantage)
- Larger equity share
- Active role in asset management

### Pros
- ✅ Access to larger, more stable assets
- ✅ Professional management
- ✅ Economies of scale
- ✅ Learn from experienced operators

### Cons
- ⚠️ Less control over decisions
- ⚠️ Depends on GP competency
- ⚠️ Capital locked up longer

### Best For
Entering larger deals while learning the market.

## Recommended Approach: Hybrid Strategy

### Phase 1: Proof of Concept (Months 1-6)
**Strategy**: Single cash-flowing SFR in Detroit suburb
- **Capital**: $70,000 - $80,000
- **Goal**: Learn market, establish systems, prove concept
- **Target**: Lincoln Park, South Rockwood, or similar

### Phase 2: Scale with Leverage (Months 6-18)
**Strategy**: BRRRR or second SFR
- **Capital**: Remaining $20,000 - $30,000 + refi proceeds
- **Goal**: Build to 2-3 properties
- **Target**: Same market for operational efficiency

### Phase 3: Portfolio Optimization (Year 2+)
**Options**:
- Sell and 1031 exchange into larger multifamily
- Continue acquiring SFRs
- Partner on larger deals
